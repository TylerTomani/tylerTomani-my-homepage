03-01_Basic input-field seperator

 By default, awk uses any amount of white space as a field seperator. It does not matter how much white space there is, white space will be the seperator.

$ awk '{print $3}'
one two three
three
$ awk '{print $3}'
one  two   three
three

-F set field seperator

 To change this behavor, use the capital F flag -F.
Whatever we place after the -F flag will be the seperator.

$ awk -F , '{print $3}'
one two, three, four
four

The space is now part of the field

 The field seperator can also be characters

$ awk -F Teeb '{print $2}'
one two TeeB three four
three four

Empty field

 If there is a blank field it will output nothing

The first line has no second field.
The second line has no first field

$ awk -F , '{print $2}'
one,,two

,two,three
two

Regular Expression seperator

 You can use regular expressions as field seperators.
This example uses either a , or ! as a seperator.

$ awk -F '[,!]' '{print $2 $3}'
one two , three ! four
 three  four
